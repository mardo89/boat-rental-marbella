By Andra Kiirkivi Founder & CEO · Boat Rental Marbella · Updated 20 May 2026 · Reviewed by local Marbella skippers

Choosing between Puerto Banús and Cabopino as your departure port for a boat charter in Marbella can shape your entire day on the water. While both are beautiful spots on the Costa del Sol, they offer very different experiences – especially when it comes to convenience, atmosphere, and access to our fleet. In this guide, we break down the key differences so you can decide which port suits your trip best.

Location and distance

Puerto Banús sits at the heart of Marbella’s Golden Mile, just west of the town centre. Cabopino is roughly 5 nautical miles further west, near the border of Marbella and Estepona. By boat, it’s a 20-minute cruise at 15 knots – barely enough time to finish your first drink. On a faster vessel like the Pershing 46 (14m, 10 pax), you can cover the distance in under 10 minutes.

Departure ports: what’s available

All our boats depart from Puerto Banús – the Astondoa 40, Azimut 39, Mangusta 80 (white, grey, and original), Azimut 58 Flybridge, Canados 86, Ferretti 94, and every other vessel in our fleet. Cabopino has a smaller marina that primarily services local fishing boats and a handful of private yachts; there are no charter operators with the range of boats we offer. If you want to charter a premium motor yacht or a spacious catamaran like the Lagoon 380 (11.6m, 10 pax), Puerto Banús is your only choice.

Parking and access

Puerto Banús has several paid parking garages (€25–€40 per day) and limited street parking. It’s busy, especially in summer, but you’re steps away from restaurants, shops, and the dock. Cabopino offers free parking in a large lot near the marina, but spaces fill up quickly on weekends. If you’re driving from Marbella town, Puerto Banús is more convenient – it’s a 10-minute drive along the A-7, whereas Cabopino is 15–20 minutes further.

Atmosphere and amenities

Puerto Banús is synonymous with luxury: designer boutiques, world-class dining at Ocean Club and Pangea, and a buzzing nightlife scene. Cabopino is quieter, with a handful of chiringuitos like Chiringuito Cabopino, a small beach, and a relaxed vibe. If you’re after a lively start to your charter – grabbing a coffee or a glass of cava before boarding – Puerto Banús wins hands down. For a chilled, low-key departure, Cabopino has its charm, but you’ll need to arrange your own transport to the marina.

Fuel and costs

All our charters include fuel, skipper, insurance, and taxes. The price you see – for example, €749 for 2 hours on an Azimut 39 – covers everything from departure to return. The fuel cost from Puerto Banús to Cabopino and back is roughly €50–€100 depending on boat size and speed. On a Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax, €4,719 for 4 hours), the fuel burn is higher, but it’s all included in the price. You won’t pay extra for distance.

Best boat for a Cabopino trip

For a short trip to Cabopino, the Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax, €749/2h) is a fantastic value – enough space for a group of friends, a comfortable flybridge, and a decent speed. If you’re celebrating something special, the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) offers a flybridge, sun pads, and a jet ski included free. For a small group, the Speedboat (7m, 6 pax) or Mariah SX21 (6.4m, 5 pax) are nimble options for a quick blast over.

Which port should you choose?

If convenience, luxury, and access to a wide fleet matter most, Puerto Banús is the clear winner. You’ll have parking, restaurants, and our full range of boats – from the Dubhe (8m, 5 pax) to the Ferretti 94 (29m, 12 pax). Cabopino is a great destination to visit during your charter, but not a practical departure point. Book your charter from Puerto Banús and enjoy a short, scenic cruise to Cabopino for lunch or a swim.

Frequently asked questions

Can I charter a boat from Cabopino?

No, we operate exclusively from Puerto Banús. All our boats depart from Puerto Banús Marina, including the Astondoa 40, Azimut 39, and Mangusta 80. Cabopino has a small marina but no charter operators with the range of boats we offer.

How long does it take to sail from Puerto Banús to Cabopino?

It takes approximately 20 minutes at 15 knots to cover the 5 NM distance. On a fast boat like the Pershing 46 (which cruises at 30+ knots), you can make it in under 10 minutes.

Is Cabopino worth visiting on a day charter?

Yes, Cabopino is a great stop for lunch at Chiringuito Cabopino or a swim at the nearby dunes beach. The marina is small but charming, and it’s a quiet alternative to the bustle of Puerto Banús.

What are the parking options at Puerto Banús vs Cabopino?

Puerto Banús has paid parking garages (approx. €25–€40 per day) and limited street parking. Cabopino offers free parking in a large lot near the marina, but it can fill up quickly in summer.

Which port has better restaurants and nightlife?

Puerto Banús is world-famous for its upscale restaurants, beach clubs, and nightlife – think Ocean Club, Pangea, and La Sala. Cabopino is quieter, with a handful of chiringuitos and a relaxed vibe.

Are there any extra costs to depart from Puerto Banús?

No, our charter prices include all port fees, fuel, skipper, and taxes. You pay the listed price – for example, €749 for 2 hours on an Azimut 39 – and that covers everything from departure to return.

Can I swim or anchor near Cabopino?

Yes, the waters off Cabopino are calm and clear, with a sandy bottom perfect for anchoring. The nearby dunes beach (Playa de Cabopino) is a popular spot for swimming and snorkelling.

What’s the best boat for a trip from Puerto Banús to Cabopino?

The Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) is ideal for a group wanting luxury and speed. For smaller groups, the Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax) offers a great balance of comfort and value at €749 for 2 hours.
