Marbella Yacht Week 2026 is the highlight of the Costa del Sol summer, drawing yacht lovers and party-goers to Puerto Banús. Whether you want a luxury day cruise or a full-on party boat, our fleet has options from €749 for 2 hours. This guide covers the best boats, pricing, and itineraries to help you plan your perfect day on the water.

When is Marbella Yacht Week 2026?

Marbella Yacht Week typically runs from late June to early September, with peak weeks in July and August. The exact 2026 dates are not yet confirmed, but the season aligns with school holidays and the best Mediterranean weather (28–32 °C sea temperatures). Book early for July and August to secure your preferred boat.

Itineraries and Stops

All charters depart from Puerto Banús. Popular destinations include:

  • Cala del Faro – A rocky cove with crystal-clear water, 30 minutes from port.
  • Cala Cortés – Sheltered bay near Cabopino, ideal for swimming and paddleboarding.
  • Cabopino Beach – Sandy beach with chiringuitos (beach bars).
  • Estepona – A scenic town 12 NM west, perfect for a lunch stop.
  • Sotogrande – 18 NM away, for those seeking a longer cruise.

What to Bring

Pack sunscreen (factor 50), a towel, sunglasses, a hat, and swimwear. You can bring your own food and extra drinks. Our standard package includes plenty of refreshments. For special dietary needs, contact us via WhatsApp.

How to Book

Book directly through our website or via WhatsApp. For Tier A boats, you can reserve online instantly. For Tier B and other boats, message us for availability and pricing. We recommend booking at least 2 weeks in advance for July and August.

Frequently asked questions

When is Marbella Yacht Week 2026?

Marbella Yacht Week typically runs from late June through early September, with peak weeks in July and August. 2026 dates are not yet announced, but the season aligns with the summer calendar. Book early for July and August to secure your preferred boat.

How much does a yacht charter cost during Marbella Yacht Week?

Prices start from €749 for 2 hours on a Tier A boat (Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39). Tier B boats like the Mangusta 80 cost €4,719 for a minimum 4 hours, including a free jet ski. Other boats are priced on request via WhatsApp. All prices include skipper, fuel, drinks, snacks, and VAT.

Which boat is best for a party during Marbella Yacht Week?

The Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) is the top party boat. It comes with a jet ski, spacious deck, and powerful engines. For smaller groups, the Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) or Pershing 46 (14m, 10 pax) are excellent choices. All depart from Puerto Banús.

Can I charter a boat for a full day?

Yes, full-day charters (8 hours) are available on Tier A boats from €2,299. For Tier B boats and others, pricing is on request. Half-day (4 hours) is also popular. All charters include a licensed skipper, fuel, drinks, and snacks.

What is included in the charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden costs. You just bring your group and sunscreen.

Where do the boats depart from?

All boats depart from Puerto Banús, Marbella's iconic luxury marina. It's easy to reach by taxi or car. From there, you can cruise to Cala del Faro, Cala Cortés, Cabopino, or even Estepona and Sotogrande on longer charters.

How many people can I bring on a charter?

Capacity varies by boat: from 5 pax (Dubhe, Mariah SX21) up to 12 pax on most larger yachts (Mangusta 80, Ferretti 94). The Azimut 39 holds 11 pax. Check each boat's details on our fleet page. All include a skipper.

Can I bring my own food and drinks?

Yes, you're welcome to bring your own food and additional drinks. Our standard package includes soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, and light snacks. For special requests, contact us via WhatsApp to arrange catering.
