Forget the crowded promenades and overpriced terrace tables. The best view of the Marbella fireworks on New Year's Eve is from the water. With a private yacht charter from Puerto Banús, you get front-row seats to the midnight display, a fully stocked bar, and a licensed skipper who handles everything. No queuing, no noise, no stress – just you, your guests, and the Costa del Sol skyline.

Why Spend NYE on a Yacht in Marbella?

Puerto Banús is the epicentre of Marbella's New Year's Eve celebrations. Every year, thousands gather along the Golden Mile and the port to watch fireworks explode over the Mediterranean. From a yacht, you avoid the shoulder-to-shoulder crowds and enjoy an uninterrupted 360° view. The bay provides a natural amphitheatre: you see the lights of La Concha mountain behind you, the glittering yachts of the marina, and the fireworks directly overhead.

Sample NYE Itinerary

  • 10:00 PM – Board your yacht at Puerto Banús. Meet your skipper, stow your bags, and get a safety briefing.
  • 10:30 PM – Cruise along the coast towards Marbella Marina. Enjoy drinks and canapés as the city lights twinkle.
  • 11:30 PM – Anchor in the bay off Puerto Banús. Prime position for the fireworks.
  • 12:00 AM – Fireworks display. Pop the cava, toast the New Year, and enjoy the show.
  • 12:30 AM – Slow cruise back to port. Music, dancing, and one last glass of wine.
  • 1:00 AM – Disembark at Puerto Banús. Walk to any of the nearby clubs or restaurants if you want to continue the night.

What About the Weather?

December in Marbella averages 15–17°C during the day and 12–15°C at night. The sea is calm in the bay, and our yachts have heated interiors. Bring a jacket or a wrap for time on deck – the fireworks look even better with a cool breeze and a warm drink in hand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we see the Marbella fireworks from the yacht?

Yes. Our yachts anchor in the bay off Puerto Banús, giving you a direct, unobstructed view of the midnight fireworks display. No crowds, no jostling – just you, your guests, and the sky lit up over the Mediterranean.

How much does a New Year's Eve yacht charter cost?

Tier A boats (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39) start at €749 for 2 hours and go up to €2,299 for 8 hours. Tier B boats like the Mangusta 80 are €4,719 for a minimum 4-hour charter and include a jet ski. All prices include skipper, drinks, snacks, insurance, and 21% VAT.

What is included in the charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, full insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). There are no hidden extras – just show up and celebrate.

How many guests can we bring?

Our fleet accommodates between 5 and 12 passengers. The Astondoa 40 and Azimut 39 take up to 9–11 guests, while the Mangusta 80 and other larger yachts carry up to 12. Check individual boat specs for exact capacity.

What time should we depart on New Year's Eve?

We recommend a departure around 10:00 PM to enjoy the coastal lights and settle in before midnight. Most charters return between 12:30 AM and 1:00 AM after the fireworks. You can customise the timing when you book.

Do we need to bring our own drinks or food?

No. All charters come with water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, and light snacks. If you want a specific bottle of champagne or a catered dinner, let us know and we can arrange it at an additional cost.

Is the yacht heated? It gets cold in December.

Yes, all our yachts have enclosed saloons with heating. You can enjoy the fireworks from the deck and retreat inside to warm up. Average December night temperatures in Marbella are around 12–15°C, so bring a jacket for the deck.

How do I book a New Year's Eve charter?

Contact us via WhatsApp or the booking form on our website. New Year's Eve is our busiest night, so we recommend booking at least two weeks in advance. We'll confirm availability and send you a secure payment link.
