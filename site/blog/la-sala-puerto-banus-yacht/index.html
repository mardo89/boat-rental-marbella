Marbella’s date-night scene just got an upgrade. The La Sala Puerto Banús yacht combo pairs a sunset cruise from Puerto Banús with dinner at one of the Costa del Sol’s most stylish beachfront restaurants. Whether you’re celebrating an anniversary, popping the question, or simply treating yourselves, this two-part evening delivers Mediterranean views, fine food, and a boat that’s entirely yours for a few hours.

Why La Sala Puerto Banús?

La Sala isn’t just another beach club. Perched on the Golden Mile between Marbella and Puerto Banús, it’s a destination in its own right. Think white sofas, candlelit tables on the sand, and a menu that runs from grilled octopus to Wagyu burgers. The crowd is international, the music is lounge, and the sunset-facing terrace makes it the logical end point for any yacht charter.

Your Sunset Charter: What to Expect

We depart from Puerto Banús marina, the glitzy harbour that’s home to superyachts, designer boutiques, and a buzzing promenade. For a date-night combo, we recommend the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) or Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax) – both start at €749 for 2 hours and include skipper, fuel, and a drinks package. That’s water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, and light snacks. No hidden extras, no upselling.

If you want more time on the water, the 4-hour option (€1,499) lets you cruise to Cala del Faro or Cabopino and back. For a real statement, the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) is available from €4,719 for 4 hours and includes a jet ski – yes, you can race across the bay before dinner.

Typical Timeline for the Perfect Evening

  • 18:00–18:30 – Board your boat at Puerto Banús. Welcome drink, safety briefing, and cast off.
  • 18:30–20:00 – Cruise west along the coast. Pass the Golden Mile, spot La Concha mountain, and anchor briefly for a swim if the weather permits.
  • 20:00–20:15 – Dock back at Puerto Banús. Taxi or walk to La Sala (5–15 minutes).
  • 20:30–22:30 – Dinner at La Sala. Terrace table, grilled sea bass, a bottle of Albariño.
  • 22:30+ – Stroll back along the beach or grab a nightcap at the marina.

How to Book the La Sala Puerto Banús Yacht Combo

Booking the charter is straightforward. You can reserve directly on our site or message us on WhatsApp. For the restaurant, we recommend booking La Sala at least a week in advance for weekend dates – they’re popular. Mention you’re coming from a yacht charter and they’ll often prioritise a terrace table.

If you’re planning a proposal or a special celebration, let us know. We can arrange champagne on board, a custom playlist, or a specific anchor spot for that golden-hour photo. Just drop us a message when you book.

What to Wear and Bring

Marbella evenings are warm from June to September (25–30°C), but the sea breeze can cool things down after sunset. A light jacket or shawl is smart. For La Sala, smart-casual works – no need for heels or a tie, but flip-flops and beachwear are best left on the boat. Bring sunscreen, sunglasses, and your phone for photos. The boat has a Bluetooth speaker, so feel free to queue your own playlist.

Why This Combo Works

Marbella has no shortage of date-night options, but the La Sala Puerto Banús yacht combo is unique. You get private time on the water, a front-row seat to the sunset, and then a seamless transition to a restaurant that knows how to do romance. No queuing for taxis, no rushed Uber rides – just a short walk or drive from dock to dinner.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is La Sala Puerto Banús?

La Sala is a chic beachfront restaurant and lounge located on the Golden Mile in Marbella, just east of Puerto Banús. It's known for its Mediterranean cuisine, stylish decor, and sunset views, making it a favourite for date nights and special occasions.

How do I combine a sunset charter with dinner at La Sala?

Book a sunset charter departing from Puerto Banús at 18:00 (2-hour option works well). After the cruise, you'll dock back at Puerto Banús, then take a 5-minute taxi or 15-minute walk along the beach to La Sala. We recommend reserving a table for 20:30 or 21:00.

What boats are available for a sunset charter in Marbella?

Our fleet includes the Astondoa 40 (€749/2h, 9 pax) and Azimut 39 (€749/2h, 11 pax) – both ideal for couples. For larger groups, the Mangusta 80 (€4,719/4h, 12 pax) includes a free jet ski. All boats depart from Puerto Banús.

What is included in the sunset charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden extras – just show up and enjoy.

Can I bring my own food or drinks on board?

Our inclusive drinks package covers a selection of beverages and snacks. You're welcome to bring additional food or drinks, but note that glass bottles are not recommended for safety. Most guests use the charter as a pre-dinner aperitif.

What time does the sunset happen in Marbella?

Sunset times vary by season: June–August around 21:30–21:45, September–October 20:00–20:30, and November–February 18:00–19:00. We adjust charter departure times accordingly so you catch the golden hour on the water.

Is La Sala suitable for a romantic dinner after a boat trip?

Absolutely. La Sala's beachfront terrace, ambient lighting, and Mediterranean menu make it a top choice for couples. The dress code is smart-casual – you'll be fine in the same outfit you wore on the boat, though a light jacket is recommended for cooler evenings.

How do I book the La Sala Puerto Banús yacht combo?

You can book the charter directly through our website or WhatsApp. For La Sala, we recommend booking a table via their website or calling ahead, especially on weekends. We can also help coordinate timing if you contact us in advance.
