The Marbella Polo Cup at the Santa María Polo Club in Sotogrande is one of the summer’s most glamorous sporting events. With a bit of planning, you can combine a morning yacht charter from Puerto Banús with an afternoon watching world-class polo – then return for a sunset cruise along the Costa del Sol. Here’s a practical same-day itinerary using our fleet.

How the Itinerary Works

The Santa María Polo Club is 30 NM south of Marbella, about a 45-minute drive from Puerto Banús. Matches typically kick off at 16:00 and finish by 18:00. That leaves the morning free for a 4-hour yacht charter (10:00–14:00) or a shorter 2-hour trip (10:00–12:00). After the match, drive back to Puerto Banús for an evening cruise along the Golden Mile. All our charters depart from Puerto Banús Marina – see our full boat list.

Best Boats for a Polo-Day Charter

For a morning charter before the polo, the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) or Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax) are ideal at €749 for 2 hours or €2,299 for 8 hours. If you want a longer day on the water, the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) costs from €4,719 for a minimum 4 hours and includes a free jet ski. All prices include skipper, fuel, drinks, snacks, insurance, and 21% Spanish VAT.

Sample Timetable: Yacht + Polo

  • 10:00–12:00 – Morning yacht charter from Puerto Banús (coast to Cabopino or Cala del Faro).
  • 12:00–12:30 – Return to marina, change, grab lunch.
  • 12:30–13:15 – Drive to Sotogrande (A-7 south).
  • 16:00–18:00 – Watch Marbella Polo Cup at Santa María Polo Club.
  • 18:00–18:45 – Drive back to Puerto Banús.
  • 19:00–21:00 – Sunset cruise along the Golden Mile or to Estepona.

This works with a 2-hour morning charter and a 2-hour evening charter, or one 4-hour charter split around the polo. Contact us via WhatsApp to arrange timing.

What’s Included in Every Charter

All our boats include a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden extras. You just bring your sun cream and polo attire.

Where to Watch the Polo

The Santa María Polo Club is at Sotogrande, a 45-minute drive from Puerto Banús. Tickets are sold by the club – we don’t sell them. The atmosphere is relaxed: think champagne, summer dresses, and linen suits. The club has a restaurant and bar. After the match, head back to the coast for a late dip at Cala Cortés or a drink at Puerto Banús.

Evening Cruise Options After the Polo

If you’ve booked a full-day charter, your skipper can take you from Puerto Banús to Estepona (10 NM west) or along the Golden Mile for sunset. The Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) or Ferretti 94 (29m, 12 pax) are great for evening entertaining. For something smaller, the Speedboat (7m, 6 pax) is a fun option. All depart from Puerto Banús.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Sotogrande from Puerto Banús?

Sotogrande is about 30 nautical miles south of Marbella, or a 40–50 minute drive by car. The Santa María Polo Club is roughly 45 minutes from Puerto Banús Marina.

What time do Marbella Polo Cup matches start?

Matches at the Santa María Polo Club usually start at 16:00 and finish around 18:00, though times can vary by day. Check the official schedule before booking.

Can I charter a yacht in the morning and still go to the polo?

Yes. Charter a boat from 10:00 to 14:00, then drive to Sotogrande for the 16:00 match. You’ll be back in Puerto Banús by 18:30 for an evening cruise.

Which boats are best for a polo-day itinerary?

The Astondoa 40 (€749/2h) or Azimut 39 (€749/2h) work well for a short morning charter. For a longer day, the Mangusta 80 (from €4,719/4h) includes a jet ski.

What’s included in the charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%).

Do I need to book the yacht and polo separately?

Yes. We only arrange the yacht charter from Puerto Banús. Polo tickets and transport to Sotogrande are your responsibility. Contact us via WhatsApp for availability.

Can I watch the polo from the yacht?

No – the Santa María Polo Club is inland, not on the coast. The yacht charter is for the morning or evening; you watch the match from the club’s grandstands.

What’s the sunset time in Marbella during polo season?

During summer (June–August), sunset is around 21:30. After the match ends at 18:00, you can enjoy a 2-hour sunset cruise back in Puerto Banús.
