Completing an Ironman 70.3 is a monumental achievement — 1.9 km swim, 90 km bike and 21.1 km run under the Andalusian sun. After crossing the finish line in Marbella, your body deserves more than a protein shake. A private yacht charter from Puerto Banús offers the ultimate recovery: cool sea breezes, gentle waves, ice-cold drinks and zero impact on tired muscles. Whether you want a pre-race calm cruise to settle nerves or a post-race recovery charter to celebrate with friends, our fleet is ready. Prices start from €749 for 2 hours and include skipper, fuel, drinks and snacks. No stress, no crowds — just the Mediterranean and your medal.

Why a Yacht Charter Works for Ironman Recovery

After 5-8 hours of intense effort, your body needs rest, hydration and gentle movement. A yacht charter delivers all three. The horizontal position on a sun lounger reduces heart rate and cortisol. The sea air is rich in negative ions, which can improve mood and reduce inflammation. And because we include water, isotonic drinks and light snacks, you can rehydrate properly without queuing at a race tent. Our skippers know the calmest spots — like the lee of La Concha mountain — where the water is flat and the sun is warm but not scorching.

Pre-Race Calm Cruise: Settle the Nerves

Many athletes find the day before the race more stressful than race day itself. A 2-hour calm cruise on the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 (€749) is the perfect antidote. Depart Puerto Banús, cruise past the Golden Mile and anchor near Cabopino for a quiet swim. No wind, no waves, no pressure. You’ll return to shore feeling centred, hydrated and ready to race. These Tier A yachts accommodate up to 11 guests, so partners and coaches can join.

Post-Race Recovery Charter: Celebrate and Recover

Once you’ve got your medal and a finisher’s photo, head straight to Puerto Banús. Our post-race recovery charters are designed for athletes who want to rest, refuel and relive the day. Choose a 4-hour charter on the Mangusta 80 (€4,719) for up to 12 people — it even includes a jet ski for those who still have energy. Or opt for the Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m) for a spacious flybridge deck perfect for stretching out. All charters include beer, cava, white wine and soft drinks, plus light snacks like olives, nuts and fresh fruit.

Best Spots for Post-Race Recovery on the Water

Our skippers know the Costa del Sol intimately. Popular recovery anchorages include Cala del Faro — a quiet cove with clear water and gentle swell — and Cala Cortés, near Estepona, where the sea is often glassy. For a longer cruise, head toward Sotogrande or the Río Verde estuary. All are within 30-45 minutes of Puerto Banús, so you spend more time relaxing than travelling.

What to Bring and What to Leave Behind

Bring your swimwear, sunscreen, a towel and your finisher’s medal for photos. Leave the energy gels and compression socks at the hotel — we provide everything you need to recover: cold drinks, shade, music and a supportive crew. If you want to swim, we have basic snorkelling gear. For non-athlete guests, the same charter works as a celebration — they can enjoy the scenery while you nap on the bow.

Book Your Ironman 70.3 Marbella Yacht Charter

Availability during Ironman week is limited, so book early. The simplest way is to browse our fleet or send a WhatsApp message with your preferred date, time and group size. We’ll confirm within hours. Pre-race calm cruises are best booked for Friday or Saturday morning; post-race recovery charters for Sunday afternoon or Monday. Whatever you choose, you’ll end your Ironman journey with salt water on your skin and a smile on your face.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I charter a yacht before the Ironman 70.3 Marbella race?

Yes. A pre-race calm cruise is ideal the day before or morning of the race. Choose a short 2-hour charter on a Tier A yacht (Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39) for €749. You’ll enjoy gentle sea air, hydration and light snacks — no stress, just relaxation near Puerto Banús.

What is the best post-race recovery charter for Ironman athletes?

After finishing, a 2- or 4-hour charter on a Tier B yacht like the Mangusta 80 (€4,719 for 4 hours) offers space for 12 people, a jet ski included free, and plenty of room to stretch, swim and rehydrate. For smaller groups, the Azimut 39 is a great option.

Are the charters suitable for non-athlete family or friends?

Absolutely. Many athletes bring supporters. All yachts accommodate up to 12 guests (except smaller boats like the Dubhe or Mariah SX21). Everyone enjoys the same inclusions: skipper, fuel, drinks and snacks.

What is included in the charter price for Ironman recovery?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden fees. Just show up and relax.

Can I book a charter for a specific time around the race schedule?

Yes. We recommend booking via WhatsApp to align with race waves, transition times or your personal schedule. Morning calm cruises and afternoon recovery charters are both popular. Let us know your preferred departure time.

Which yacht is best for a small group of Ironman participants?

For up to 5 athletes, the Dubhe (8m) or Mariah SX21 (6.4m) are cosy and affordable. For up to 8, the Bandido (11.5m) or Red Tide (11m) work well. Larger groups of 10-12 should consider the Azimut 39, Astondoa 40 or Mangusta 80.

Is it possible to swim or snorkel during the recovery charter?

Yes. The skipper will anchor at a calm spot like Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés where you can swim in the Mediterranean. Life jackets and basic snorkelling gear are available on request. Post-race swims are a favourite recovery activity.

How do I book and what is the cancellation policy?

Book via WhatsApp or our website. A deposit secures your slot. Cancellation policy varies by yacht and season — ask at booking. We always recommend confirming at least 48 hours ahead for Ironman week to guarantee availability.
