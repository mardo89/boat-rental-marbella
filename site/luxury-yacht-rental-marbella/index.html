Luxury Yacht Rental Marbella 2026: Superyachts & Crewed Charters

Luxury yacht rental in Marbella means stepping onto an 18-to-30-metre crewed yacht at Puerto Banús, being greeted by a stewardess with a chilled glass, and handing the day over to a professional crew while you and your guests do nothing but enjoy it. Day charters start at €2,400 and scale up to €18,000 for the largest superyachts. Weekly charters begin around €25,000 plus expenses.

Where the line is

Yacht charter in Marbella exists on a continuum, and "luxury" is the top end of it. Practical breakpoints:

  • 10-15 m motor yacht: private day boat. Captain only. Comfortable but not luxury. Covered on the yacht charter Marbella page.
  • 18-22 m crewed yacht: entry-level luxury. Captain + stewardess. Hospitality-fitted interior, real galley, fold-out swim platform, water toys included. This is where most luxury bookings sit.
  • 24-30 m yacht: superyacht. Full crew (captain, mate, chef, stewardess). Multiple staterooms, chef-prepared meals, on-board tender. Day charter possible but most owners prefer multi-day.
  • 30 m+ megayacht: charter market thins out fast. Usually weekly or longer, crew of 5-10, full-board.

Weekly charter and the APA model

Multi-day and weekly charters on 24 m+ yachts use the global APA system. The base charter fee covers the yacht, captain and crew salaries. On top of that you pre-pay an Advance Provisioning Allowance (APA) of 25-30% which funds fuel, marina fees, food, drink and any extra crew expenses during the trip. Captain accounts for every euro and refunds the unused portion at the end. A 24 m yacht for a week in low season: ~€25,000 + ~€7,000 APA. Same yacht in August: ~€40,000 + €10,000 APA.

The crew and what they actually do

Captain: commands the yacht — navigation, anchoring, all decisions affecting safety. Holds at least a 200 GT commercial licence. Your single point of contact for the trip.

Stewardess: guest service. Welcomes you on board, mixes drinks, plates the food, handles cabin turn-down on overnight charters, keeps the saloon and decks tidy. The stewardess role on a luxury yacht is the difference between "nice boat" and "luxury experience".

Chef: from 24 m up. You fill in a preference sheet before the charter — favourite cuisines, dietary requirements, allergies — and the chef builds a tailored menu for the trip. Provisioning is done from local markets (Mercado de Puerto Banús, La Cañada).

Deckhand / mate: handles lines, fenders, tender operation, water-toys deployment, and keeps the exterior immaculate. Often a deck cadet building sea time toward their captain qualification.

Where you can go

Day charters cover the Costa del Sol — Sotogrande in the west, Cabopino in the east, with the Golden Mile coast in between. A long day with an early start reaches Gibraltar (100 NM round trip, only viable on flat-sea days). Overnight charters open up Tarifa, the Strait, and short crossings to Tangier or Ceuta in Morocco (extra paperwork — captain handles). Weekly charters often run a Marbella → Gibraltar → Ceuta → Tarifa → Marbella loop, or push further to Cádiz or the Algarve.

Frequently asked questions

What counts as a luxury yacht in Marbella?

Generally 18 metres and above with captain plus stewardess minimum, hospitality-fitted interior, and water toys. Above 24 m you're in superyacht territory with chef and multiple staterooms.

How much is a luxury yacht charter per day?

€2,400 for an 18 m yacht in low season, up to €18,000 for a megayacht in August. Weekly starts ~€25,000 for a 24 m yacht plus APA expenses.

What does 'crewed charter' actually include?

Captain (navigation), stewardess (guest service), chef from 24 m up (custom menus), deckhand (lines, tender, water toys). Tipping 10-15% of charter price at the end is customary.

Can we go anywhere?

Day: Estepona to Cabopino; long days reach Gibraltar. Overnight: Tarifa, Strait of Gibraltar, short Morocco crossings. Weekly: Algarve or Cádiz possible.

How far ahead do I need to book?

Day charters: 2-4 weeks for peak Saturdays. Weekly charters on specific yachts in August: 3-6 months. The 30 m+ market is small (8-10 yachts) and sells out early.
