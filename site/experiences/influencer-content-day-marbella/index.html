Why choose a yacht for your content day?

Marbella’s coastline delivers backdrops that few studios can match: the whitewashed port of Puerto Banús, the silhouette of La Concha mountain, and the deep blue of the Alboran Sea. A yacht gives you multiple sets in one charter – sunpad, bow, flybridge, and cockpit – all bathed in natural light. With a licensed skipper handling navigation, you focus entirely on framing the shot.

Best times for natural light

Golden hour in Marbella runs from about 17:30 to 19:30 (May–September). The sun sets behind La Concha, casting warm tones across the port. For a softer, more even light, book a morning slot from 10:00 to 13:00. Afternoon charters (13:00–16:00) give you high sun and deep shadows – great for dramatic fashion shots.

Popular shooting spots near Puerto Banús

Most charters head west along the coast. Top anchor points include:

  • Cala del Faro – small cove with rocky cliffs, 15 minutes from port
  • Cabopino – sandy beach and dunes, 20 minutes east
  • Estepona coast – 30 minutes west, quieter waters
  • Río Verde – estuary backdrop, 10 minutes

Your skipper will suggest the best spot based on wind and sea conditions.

What to bring for your shoot

Frequently asked questions

What is included in an influencer content day yacht charter?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). You just bring your camera and talent – we handle the rest.

Which yachts are best for content creation?

The Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) offers a huge sunpad and open flybridge for wide shots. The Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) has a spacious aft deck. For smaller groups, the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) is photogenic and affordable.

Can we bring extra equipment like drones or lighting?

Yes, you can bring drones, lighting, and other gear. The skipper will help you find safe spots for drone launches. Please inform us in advance so we can confirm deck space and any restrictions.

What time of day is best for filming on the water?

Late afternoon charters (16:00–19:00) give golden hour light over La Concha mountain. Morning trips (10:00–13:00) offer calm seas and fewer boats near Puerto Banús. We recommend booking 2–4 weeks ahead for prime slots.

How many people can join a content day shoot?

Our fleet ranges from 5 guests (Dubhe, Mariah SX21) up to 12 guests (Mangusta 80, Ferretti 94). For a crew of 4–6 plus talent, the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 (11 pax) work well.

Do I need a special permit to film on the yacht?

No special permit is required for personal or commercial content creation aboard our yachts. The skipper handles all navigation and safety. If you plan to use the yacht for a paid brand collaboration, just let us know in advance.

What is the cancellation policy for a content day?

We require 48 hours' notice for a full refund. For weather cancellations, we reschedule or refund 100%. No-shows or same-day cancellations are non-refundable.

Can we stop for swimming or water sports during the shoot?

Absolutely. Many content days include B-roll of guests swimming or using the free jet ski on Mangusta 80 charters. Popular stops include Cala del Faro and Cabopino beach.
