Estepona sits 18 nautical miles west of Marbella along the Costa del Sol — about 25 minutes by road and a popular alternative charter departure for groups who don't want the Puerto Banús crowds. Boat rental from Estepona works as either direct charters with the 5–7 yachts based at Puerto de Estepona, or repositioning our Marbella fleet (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39, Mangusta 80, Lagoon 380) west for the day. This page covers both, plus the three best routes west from Puerto de Estepona — Sotogrande, Gibraltar, and the Marbella coast.

Why charter from Estepona

  • Quieter marina. Puerto de Estepona has ~440 berths vs Puerto Banús's 915. Boarding is calm, no superyacht-tender traffic, no club-crowd noise.
  • Free parking. Avenida de España along the marina has free street spots almost any day outside mid-August. Puerto Banús charges €3.20/hr in the marina car park.
  • Closer to Gibraltar and Sotogrande. 15 NM to Gibraltar (vs 33 NM from Marbella), 10 NM to Sotogrande (vs 28 NM). Day-trip routes west are 1–1.5 hours shorter.
  • Lower pricing. Estepona berth costs are ~25% lower than Banús; we pass that through.

Trade-off: Estepona has fewer on-marina restaurants and beach clubs. If you want pre-charter lunch at Nikki Beach or post-charter cocktails at Ocean Club, Puerto Banús is the call.

What departs from Puerto de Estepona

Five to seven yachts are active in charter from Estepona in 2026:

  • Two 8–10 m motor yachts (day-charter, half-day, sunset).
  • One 14 m Sunseeker (full-day premium charters, multi-day to Gibraltar / Tarifa).
  • Two Beneteau sailing yachts 36–42 ft.
  • Jet ski operator at the beach end (Sea-Doo fleet, hourly rentals).
  • Several license-free 5 m / 15 hp boats for self-drive.

For specific Marbella-based yachts — the Mangusta 80 (24 m sport yacht), the Lagoon 380 catamaran, or our 12.5 m Astondoa 40 / Azimut 39 — we reposition west for the day. Repositioning fee €220–€350 depending on direction and yacht size.

Three routes west from Estepona

Sotogrande (10 NM — half-day)

The closest serious destination. 35 minutes cruising at 18 knots. Pass the Estepona lighthouse, the rocky shore at Punta Plata, and into Sotogrande Bay. Lunch options: anchor off the inner beach and stay aboard, or moor at Marina de Sotogrande (€60–€140 for a 3-hour visit slip) and walk ashore to Ke Restaurant or Trocadero. Return by 4pm.

Gibraltar (15 NM — full day)

The flagship Estepona day charter. 50 minutes each way at cruising speed. Pass Sotogrande, La Línea, around Europa Point with the Rock looming above. Moor at Marina Bay or Ocean Village for 2–3 hours ashore (lunch at The Tipsy Lounge, walk up to Apes' Den, duty-free shopping). Return by 5pm. Best season May–October. Allow extra time mid-August for marina berth availability.

Marbella coast (12 NM east — full day or half-day)

Reverse direction — east to Puerto Banús and the La Concha mountain backdrop. 40 minutes cruising. Anchor off Playa de Bonita or in Puerto Banús for the lunch crowd. Return calmer in late afternoon. Useful for groups staying in Estepona but wanting to see Marbella from the water.

Practical: parking, transport, old town

  • Free street parking on Avenida de España alongside the marina — usually open.
  • Paid car park at marina entrance €1.80/hr if street is full.
  • Taxi rank at marina gate. Cabify works in Estepona.
  • Estepona town centre 8 minutes' walk — narrow whitewashed streets, the orange-tree square (Plaza de las Flores), 50+ painted murals along the alleys.
  • Pre-charter coffee: Café Java on Calle Real, or El Pimpi for breakfast.
  • Post-charter dinner: Restaurante Beach House on the seafront, or La Casa del Rey in the old town.

Frequently asked questions

Why charter from Estepona rather than Puerto Banús?

Three reasons: (1) Less congested marina — easier boarding, quieter dock walk. (2) Easier parking — free street parking near Puerto de Estepona vs paid in Banús. (3) Closer to Gibraltar and Sotogrande, so day-trip routes west are 1–1.5 hours shorter return. Trade-off: Estepona has fewer on-marina restaurants if you want a pre-charter lunch.

What's the Estepona charter fleet like?

Five to seven active yachts based at Puerto de Estepona: two 8–10 m motor yachts, one 14 m Sunseeker, two Beneteau sailing yachts, and an active jet ski operator at the beach end. For specific yachts (Mangusta 80, Lagoon 380 catamaran), we reposition from Marbella for a €220–€350 fee.

How long does it take to reach Gibraltar from Estepona?

15 nautical miles on a direct line — 50 minutes at 18 knots on a planing motor yacht, 2.5 hours under sail. Day-charter to Gibraltar from Estepona is comfortable in 7–8 hours: 50 minutes out, 3 hours moored at Gibraltar (lunch ashore at Marina Bay), 50 minutes back, plus swim stops on the way.

What about Sotogrande?

10 NM west — 35 minutes at cruising speed. Best as a half-day charter: cruise to Sotogrande's outer breakwater, lunch at anchor or moor at Marina de Sotogrande for an hour ashore, return. 4-hour comfortable, 6-hour relaxed. Sotogrande hosts the Costa's largest superyacht marina — worth seeing from the water even if you don't dock.

How much for a half-day charter from Estepona?

€800–€1,400 on a 10–14 m yacht. €1,200–€2,200 on a 16–18 m. Lower than equivalent Puerto Banús pricing because Estepona berth costs are lower (we pass it through). Adds €200–€350 if you want a specific Marbella-based yacht repositioned.

Is Estepona old town worth visiting before or after the charter?

Yes. Estepona's old town has been heavily restored — narrow whitewashed streets, the orange-tree square (Plaza de las Flores), and 50+ painted murals along the main alleys. 30 minutes' walk from the marina. Best pre-charter coffee stop: Café Java on Calle Real.

Can I do a one-way charter to Marbella or Sotogrande?

Yes. One-way charters from Estepona to Marbella (12 NM) or to Sotogrande (10 NM) are possible — useful if you're staying at one and want to relocate. Pricing is half-day rate + €120–€180 captain return fee. We coordinate.

What's parking like at Puerto de Estepona?

Free street parking on Avenida de España along the marina seafront — usually available except mid-August. Paid car park at the marina entrance €1.80/hr. Most renters park free. Compare to Puerto Banús where the marina car park is €3.20/hr.
