Málaga sits 40 nautical miles east of Marbella along the Costa del Sol — a 1h15 cruise on our 12.5 m motor yachts, or a 35-minute drive by road. Boat rental in Málaga works two ways through us: direct charters with Málaga-based yachts berthed at Muelle Uno, or repositioning our Marbella fleet (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39, Mangusta 80, Lagoon 380) to Málaga for the day. This page covers both, plus the three best day-charter routes from Málaga port.

Where charters depart from in Málaga

Muelle Uno (officially Marina del Puerto de Málaga) is the main charter departure. Built on the old fishing dock and pedestrianised in 2013, it runs along the south side of the harbour from the Pompidou Centre west to the lighthouse. Practical bits:

  • 5-minute walk from Calle Larios (Old Town) and Atarazanas market.
  • Public car park underneath the marina (€2.50/hr, 24/7 access).
  • Taxi rank at the marina gate; Cabify and Uber work in central Málaga.
  • Train station (Málaga María Zambrano) 1.5 km — €4 in a taxi, 18 minutes on foot.
  • Charter desks open from 9am; check-in for a 10am charter is 9:45.

Some larger yachts dock at Muelle 2 (the commercial / superyacht slips) — your booking confirmation will tell you which slip.

Three day-charter routes from Málaga port

East: Málaga → Nerja (Maro caves)

Best full-day route. 25 NM each way along the Axarquía coast. Pass Rincón de la Victoria, Vélez-Málaga, then the dramatic Maro cliffs and the Cueva del Tesoro caves. Swim stop at Cala del Cañuelo. Lunch at anchor off Nerja or stop at Marinas de Nerja. 6.5 hours afloat, back at Málaga by 5pm.

West: Málaga → Marbella (Puerto Banús)

40 NM each way, full day. Pass Torremolinos, Benalmádena, Fuengirola, Calahonda. Lunch in Puerto Banús — moor at the visitor dock, walk to Sinatra Bar or Astral Beach Club. Return mid-afternoon. Best for groups who want the 'see the Costa del Sol' day rather than a swim-heavy itinerary.

Bay loop (4 hours)

Half-day charter. Out to the Málaga lighthouse, west along Torremolinos coast, anchor at La Carihuela for a swim, return via the Pompidou Centre seafront. Comfortable for groups with children or non-sailors who don't want a long day on the water.

What's included on a Málaga charter

  • Licensed skipper (Patrón de Yate / Capitán de Yate).
  • Fuel within agreed cruising area.
  • Drinks: beer, wine, soft drinks, water, ice.
  • Snacks: chips, olives, charcuterie board.
  • Towels, snorkels (where appropriate), Bluetooth music.
  • Despacho de salida (sailing permit) filed with Málaga port authority.

Optional add-ons: catered lunch (€35–€55/pp), jet ski tow (€280 for 4h), paddle boards (€40/board/day), professional photographer (€450 half-day).

Frequently asked questions

Can I charter the same yacht in Málaga that you list in Marbella?

Yes — our 12.5 m Astondoa 40 and Azimut 39 reposition to Málaga's Muelle Uno on 24-hour notice. Repositioning fee €280–€450 covers fuel + skipper time for the 40 NM each-way hop. Worth it for a half-day or full-day charter; not worth it for a 2-hour sunset cruise.

Where do charters depart from in Málaga?

Muelle Uno (Marina del Puerto de Málaga) — the urban marina next to the Pompidou Centre and Plaza de la Marina. 5-minute walk from the Old Town and Atarazanas market. Parking is at the public car park under the marina (€2.50/hr). Some larger yachts dock at Muelle 2; we direct you to the right slip 24 hours ahead.

How much for a day charter from Málaga?

Charter rates the same as Marbella: €749 for a 2h sunset cruise, €1,499 for a 4h half-day, €2,299 for an 8h full day on our 12.5 m motor yachts. Add €280–€450 repositioning if we bring the yacht from Marbella. Direct-from-Málaga operators we work with charge €450–€1,800 with no repositioning fee.

What can you see in a day from Málaga?

Three good routes. (1) East to Nerja: 25 NM each way, full day — Frigiliana cliffs, Maro caves, swim stop. (2) West to Marbella: 40 NM each way, full day — La Concha mountain backdrop, lunch in Puerto Banús. (3) Bay loop: 4 hours, Málaga lighthouse, Torremolinos coast, return via Muelle Uno. Pick when you book.

What's included?

Same inclusions as our Marbella charters: licensed skipper, fuel (within agreed area), drinks (beer, wine, soft drinks, water, ice), snacks, towels, music system. Extra catering, jet ski tow, paddle boards — add-on at booking.

Can I take the boat without a skipper?

Bareboat charter from Málaga is possible on smaller boats (license-free 5 m / 15 hp) and on yachts up to 12 m if you hold PER + sailing endorsement. For our 12 m+ motor yachts we provide the skipper. Booking confirms based on licence verification.

What's the booking process?

WhatsApp +358 400 406 194 with date, group size, departure preference (Marbella or Málaga). We confirm yacht availability and pricing within 60 minutes. 30% deposit holds the date; balance 7 days before charter. Cancellation policy: full refund if 14+ days out, 50% if 7–14, no refund inside 7.

Does Málaga have its own dedicated charter fleet, or am I getting a Marbella yacht?

Both. Málaga has 4–6 yachts based at Muelle Uno (medium-sized motor yachts and one Beneteau sailboat). For larger or specific yachts (Mangusta 80, Lagoon 380 catamaran, license-free boats), we reposition from Marbella. We tell you upfront which yacht and which marina the booking applies to.
