A honeymoon charter in Marbella is a different beast from a sunset cruise — usually 2 nights, not 2 hours. This page covers the actual playbook: which boat to book for an overnight, where we anchor for the night (rare in Spain — there are rules), how the catering works at anchor, and what makes the day-after morning unique.

Where you actually sleep

Important to set expectations: Spanish maritime regulation prohibits overnight anchoring inside marina-controlled zones (Puerto Banús, Marbella Marina). For overnight stays, we either:

  • Stay at the marina berth at Puerto Banús — air-conditioned cabin, electricity, water hook-up, marina security, walking distance to dinner. Most popular and simplest.
  • Anchor offshore at Río Verde or Cala del Faro — only in flat-sea forecasts (under 8-knot wind). Quieter, more romantic, but you'll feel the boat move. Permit zones are limited; we confirm 24h before.
  • Move to a different marina for the night — Sotogrande or Estepona. Adds €200–€400 berth fee. The novel "second port" feel.

The two-night romantic itinerary

Day 1 — arrival

  1. 16:00 Check in at the boat, champagne in the saloon.
  2. 17:00 Sunset cruise to Río Verde. Anchor. Swim if warm enough.
  3. 19:30 Catered dinner on board (catering boats over from the dock) or short dinghy to a beach club.
  4. 22:00 Return to Puerto Banús for the night. Master cabin made up; air-con on.

Day 2 — full day

  1. 09:00 Breakfast on board (we cater fresh fruit, pastries, eggs, coffee). Eat in the cockpit.
  2. 10:30 Cast off. Cruise west to Estepona or east to Cabopino dunes. Two anchor stops for swim + snorkel.
  3. 14:00 Lunch at a beach restaurant (we tender you in). El Garum, Trocadero Sotogrande, Buddha Beach are easy.
  4. 17:00 Sunset slow cruise back to Puerto Banús. Optional dinner ashore — Casanis, La Sala, Sea Grill.

Day 3 — morning

  1. 10:00 Late check-out — breakfast on board if you want.
  2. 11:00 Crew handles the boat handover.

Frequently asked questions

Can we anchor overnight away from any marina?

Only in flat-sea forecasts and only at permitted anchorages (Río Verde, Cala del Faro). Spanish law restricts overnight anchoring near beaches and marinas. We confirm the night-before based on the weather forecast — most honeymoons end up at a marina berth, which is comfortable and air-conditioned.

How does the bathroom situation work?

Each cabin has its own marine head with shower. Hot water from the boat's heater. Toilet flushes to a holding tank emptied at the marina.

Will we be alone on the boat?

The skipper sleeps in the crew cabin (separate, forward) on multi-night charters. On the Astondoa or Azimut, you have the master cabin to yourselves; the skipper is on call but invisible. On the Mangusta 80, captain + deckhand have their own quarters and only appear at meals or when you request.

What if we get seasick?

Marina-overnight is virtually motionless. Anchored-offshore overnight you'll feel some rocking. We pick anchors carefully; if anyone is prone to seasickness we recommend the marina-stay option.

Can we have dinner delivered to the boat?

Yes — local catering partners deliver to the dock at Puerto Banús. We coordinate. €120/couple for a multi-course catered dinner.
